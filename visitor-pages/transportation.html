Transportation

Arriving in Taniti

Most visitors arrive by air, as Taniti is served by a small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. The island is currently expanding its airport, and in the coming years, larger jets will be able to land.

For those arriving by sea, a small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay once a week, allowing visitors to explore the island for a short stay.

Public & Private Buses

Public buses operate within Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, making it easy to get around the city.

Private buses provide service to destinations across the rest of the island.

Taxis & Rental Cars

Taxis are readily available within Taniti City for quick and convenient travel.

A local rental agency near the airport offers rental cars for those who want to explore at their own pace.

Bike Rentals

Visitors can rent bikes and helmets from multiple vendors.

Helmets are required by law, so all rentals include them.

Walking & Exploring on Foot

Taniti City is flat and very walkable, making it easy to explore without a vehicle.

Many tourists stay in the Merriton Landing area, which is best explored on foot due to its shops, restaurants, and entertainment options.
